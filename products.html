Scollioexercise

I’ve always loved being an inventor, creator, and designer, and from a 5-key piano built on a breadboard to technologically-advanced dog collars, I'm constantly working to create a multitude of useful gadgets. Of all my creations, my pride and joy is Scollioexercise; I spent countless hours in collaboration with members of my robotics team to build this device, and together we created a product that can change lives.

Scoliosis is a medically significant sideways curvature of the spine which affects around 6-9 million people in the US. This condition comes in forms ranging from a minor curvature of the spine, to extreme cases where the spine has a curvature over 80°. The best way to prevent the progression of scoliosis is exercise, but when hindered by scoliosis, it becomes difficult to get proper exercise and prevent progression.

My team and I decided to try to find a way to make exercise safer for people with scoliosis. As an outside hitter (spiker) on my high school’s volleyball team, I have seen firsthand the impact exercise has on my mental and physical well-being. Even more important for those in the constant discomfort of scoliosis, however, are the endorphins that exercising releases. Endorphins are natural painkillers and provide a sensation of calm following a workout. Exercising also boosts dopamine, serotonin and norepinephrine levels, which ultimately leads to a happier state of mind and improved mental health—which I have personally felt after long volleyball practices! Because many with scoliosis don’t have access to safely monitored exercise that won’t injure their spines, they are left in an endless cycle of pain, impacting their mental health as well.

My team and I targeted our efforts to help provide an affordable and accessible way for scoliosis patients to live a happier and healthier life. Through this process our product was born: Scollioexercise. Scollioexercise is a smart vest that patients with scoliosis can wear while exercising, after consultation with their physiotherapists, that will inform them through a mobile app about any strain on their spine. I decided to propose a system that contained multiple point sensors between sections of the vest to detect movements of the spine and track if the tension could injure the spine. 

After refining our idea, we sought out medical counseling from physiotherapists and doctors who work with patients living with scoliosis, and they encouraged and helped us improve. Eventually, we submitted Scollioexercise for consideration for the FIRST Innovation Challenge and were selected as one of just 20 teams globally to move on to the Global Innovation Challenge. We were chosen for the ingenuity of our product, business model, and product research, and we couldn’t be more excited for the impact we can make. Fast forward to 2023, our team is still actively working on the process of applying for a patent and getting closer to releasing our product to the world. We can’t wait to see Scollioexercise in action, showing the power of creativity to help people!
